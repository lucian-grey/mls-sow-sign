Agreement Terms

1. Purpose

This Statement of Work Agreement records acceptance of Lucas's monthly retainer work with Jero / My Last Straw. The related Proof proposal explains the commercial rationale and priority stack.

2. Engagement Model

  • Monthly build and operations retainer.
  • SGD $3,800 per active monthly cycle.
  • About 20 hours per week, about 80 hours per month.
  • Month-to-month, renewed one active cycle at a time.
  • The prior SGD $2,000 payment was already paid separately and is not credited against this monthly retainer.

3. Priority Stack

  1. Notes vending machine due by 1 Jul.
  2. Payroll and commission automation target 7-10 Jul.
  3. Trial-to-paid funnel automation.
  4. CRM dedupe and source-of-truth cleanup.
  5. Business data layer for Jero and his agent.
  6. Landing and pricing pages.
  7. Later workspace migration and hardening.

4. Delivery and Acceptance

Deliverables may include software, automations, schemas, dashboards, documentation, prompts, API/export views, walkthroughs, and configuration changes. A deliverable is accepted when Client confirms acceptance, uses it in production, or does not provide specific written rejection reasons within five business days after delivery or demo.

5. Client Dependencies

Client is responsible for timely access, decisions, source data, payroll/commission rules, account permissions, and approvals. Delays in dependencies may shift timelines or priorities.

6. Payment, Scope, and Changes

The retainer reserves Lucas's capacity for the active monthly cycle. Third-party costs, subscriptions, ad spend, transaction fees, and platform upgrades are Client expenses unless agreed otherwise in writing. Material changes may be re-prioritized, swapped into the stack, or handled in a later retainer cycle.

7. Data, Confidentiality, and IP

Client remains responsible for lawful handling of business and personal data. Each party will protect non-public business and operational information. Client owns Client data and business-specific deliverables after relevant fees are paid; Lucas retains reusable tools, templates, generic code patterns, know-how, prompts, and methods developed outside or across client work.

8. No Guaranteed Business Outcome

Lucas will use reasonable skill and care, but does not guarantee specific revenue, conversion rates, ad performance, platform approval, deliverability, third-party uptime, or student/customer behavior.

9. Liability, Termination, and Law

To the maximum extent permitted by law, Lucas's total liability is limited to fees paid for the active monthly cycle giving rise to the claim. Either party may choose not to renew the next monthly cycle. Unless otherwise agreed in writing, this SOW is governed by the laws of Singapore.
